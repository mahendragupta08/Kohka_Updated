Corona-Virus Relief

FROM THE FRONTLINE



We have been working tirelessly on the forefront to not only spread information about the Covid-19 pandemic to the innermost areas of the country but are also engaged in Mumbai and Nagpur to fight the virus with the necessary essentials since 2020.

We urge all our supporters to donate to our ongoing fundraisers to ensure we can continue our relief efforts.

COOKED MEALS FOR MIGRANT WORKERS


The nation-wide lockdown in India left thousands of migrant workers and labourers displaced without any transportation and food resources.

As of now, we have provided freshly cooked hot meals to more than 20,000 stranded migrants crossing the Madhya Pradesh border. started with distributing food packages to the 1500+ migrants crossing the Madhya Pradesh border.

TRANSGENDER COMMUNITY



Stripped off any livelihood and with no employability due to social distancing, Mumbai's transgender community's survival has been under threat since the past 4 months.

We have provided them with more than 800 monthly ration kits, 2000 face masks, 3500 bottles of hand sanitizers, 2 oximeters and 2 infrared thermometers.

POLICE DEPARTMENT : MUMBAI & NAGPUR


To ensure our country's frontline workers warriors have the neccesary resources to fight Covid-19, we have donated 20,000 face masks, 6000 face shields 20 pulse oximeters to the Mumbai Police Departments.

Nagpur Police Department has been given 5000 cotton face masks, 3001 face shields and 5000 hand sanitizers.
Seoni, Madhya Pradesh have been donated 35 hand sanitizer machines and 100ml sanitizer bottles.

SANITARY & HYGIENE KIT


Periods don't stop for a pandemic. Grocery stores in rural India face a shortage of period essentials, the threat of having an unsafe, unhygienic and undignified monthly experience is a threat for women and young girls.

We have distributed more than 8,630 Hygiene Kits across multiple villages in Seoni District,
Madhya Pradesh.

RATION KIT DISTRIBUTION


The country, especially it's innermost areas are facing a dangerous shortage of supply of essential commodities, including food, soap, etc. ever since the lockdown.

Till date, we have provided monthly ration kits for 1,800 families in Seoni District,
Madhya Pradesh over the span of four months.

We have also transported 450 ration kits to Naxalite areas in Maharashtra through the Nagpur Police Department.

PPE KITS FOR CREMATORIUM WORKERS



Crematorium workers have been risking their lives while dealing with the funeral rites of the increasing number of people who are losing their lives to Covid-19.

Through Mumbai's municipal body BMC's K West Ward, 200 PPE Kits were distributed to Amboli and Oshiwara crematorium workers.

FACE MASK PRODUCTION


With the combined efforts of the Seoni District Collector, the innmates of Seoni Juvenile Correction Home have been employed to produce face masks especially needed in this time.
The innmates have themselves been provided with face masks and are working in small groups at a distance to prioritise their health.
More than 3000 masks have been produced in a span of one month. 600 masks have been donated to Seoni Nagarpalika. It is currently being distributed for free to the villages of Seoni district and other displaced individuals.

SPREADING AWARENESS



Awareness building programmes for the villagers of Seoni district, Madhya Pradesh were kicked off with a talk by a reputable gynecologist on Women's Day (8th March, 2020).

We have covered 30 villages and spoken about the pandemic, its risks and how to be equipped in our fight against it. There have been no tested cases.

SEX WORKERS - MUMBAI

Sex workers and their families in Lesser-known red light areas like Sonapur, Ghatkopar West in Mumbai have been unable to gain access to government provisions in the midst of the lockdown.
At the start of the festive season in our country, we were able to provide some relief by donating and distributing 50 ration kits with 2 face masks and 2 hand santizers to each family.
The future seems bleak for such populations as they have been surviving without any sources of livelihood for the past 4 months.
We thank Prerna Foundation for organising and facilitating this donation drive.
